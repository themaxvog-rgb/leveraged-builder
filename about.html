Where I started

I was a photographer who learned to sell.

I started with a camera and no business model. Photography led me into marketing — understanding how brands communicate, how visuals drive decisions, how attention is the real currency. That turned into running campaigns, then managing brands, then building systems for clients who had no idea what they were doing online.

Eventually I built an agency. Brand management, video production, corporate clients in Brazil. Good revenue. Good work. But a model that required my constant presence to function — which meant it didn't scale, it just moved.

The turning point

AI didn't save me time. It changed what I was building.

When I started using AI seriously — not as a gimmick, but as infrastructure — something shifted. Not "I write emails faster now." More like: I can design systems that produce output without me in them. The agency started running on workflows instead of hours.

That's when I made the bet. I'm building three things simultaneously: the agency (to fund everything), a business coaching program (to help people make the same shift I did), and this media brand — to document the whole thing, live, in public.

Why build in public

The most useful thing I can do is show the real process.

Most business content is written in retrospect by people who've already won. They smooth out the failures, compress the timeline, and package it into a neat framework. That's not useful to someone in the middle of building.

The Leveraged Builder exists to be the resource I wish I had. Not "here's how someone made $10M eventually" — but "here's what week 4 actually looked like, with the exact numbers, the mistakes, and the system I used."

I have real stakes. Real deadlines. Real failures. And I'm documenting all of it.

What I believe

The operating principles.

  • 01
    AI is infrastructure, not a shortcut The people winning aren't using AI to do tasks faster. They're using it to eliminate entire categories of work from their week.
  • 02
    One person with systems beats a team without them Scale isn't about headcount. It's about how many outputs your systems produce per hour you actually work.
  • 03
    Where you live is a choice, not a ceiling São Paulo to New York, I'm building for a global market. Geography should determine your lifestyle, not your income.
  • 04
    Transparency compounds The most valuable audience is built on trust. Real numbers, real failures, real process. No polish, no pretense.
  • 05
    Deadlines make everything real I have one: $1 million before I turn 30 in July 2027. Everything I do either moves toward that or it doesn't.
